### Opening

Async/await is the obvious clean-up for callback spaghetti, but most shipping apps can’t flip a switch without breaking behavior or cancelling incorrectly. This issue lays out a pragmatic, low-risk path for migrating completion handlers to Swift’s structured concurrency without turning a release into an incident.

---

### This Week's Big Story

**Migrate Completion Handlers to Swift Structured Concurrency**

Structured concurrency simplifies reasoning about async flows, but replacing completion handlers across a large codebase changes observable semantics in ways tests and users will notice. The engineering problem isn’t syntax — it’s preserving cancellation, resource lifetimes, and telemetry while you roll changes out. Read on for a step-by-step approach that keeps your surface API stable while you convert internals incrementally.
