Opening

Delegates in UIKit are stable and familiar — and they quietly cost you composability and sane cancellation semantics as screens grow complex. Teams are starting small: move a delegate at a time into Swift Concurrency, keep the rest intact, and gain structured cancellation without ripping your codebase apart.

This Week's Big Story

Migrating UIKit Delegates to Swift Concurrency Tasks

Delegate callbacks nest nicely until they don't: mixing lifecycle ownership, cancellation, and multiple producers quickly becomes a maintenance tax. Swift Concurrency gives you explicit tasks, continuations, and AsyncStream to model those flows, but the migration is an incremental design problem — adapter shapes, lifecycle visibility, and back-compat matter more than clever one-liners. Do it view-scoped, test the adapter lifecycle, and prefer hybrid approaches where hot loops or latency needs keep the native delegate.
