Opening

Custom layouts are quietly becoming an engineering decision, not just a UI flourish. Teams are juggling accessibility, Dynamic Type, and unpredictable content sizes—those edge cases expose when stacks and GeometryReader hacks stop being maintainable.

This Week's Big Story

Custom SwiftUI Layouts with the Layout Protocol

When your UI needs deterministic spatial rules across variable content and accessibility settings, the Layout protocol gives you the explicit measure/place hooks you actually need. It centralizes layout math so teams can reason about performance and reuse instead of scattering ad-hoc constraints. If you care about predictable behavior, fewer layout bugs, and testing before rollout, extracting a Layout is worth the cost — but it’s not always the right move.
